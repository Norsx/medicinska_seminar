# Force Tracking Control Method for Robotic Ultrasound Scanning System under Soft Uncertain Environment

* **ID:** A1
* **Authors:** Jinlei Jiang, Jingjing Luo, Hongbo Wang, Xiuhong Tang, Fan Nian, Lizhe Qi
* **Year:** 2024
* **DOI:** 10.3390/act13020062
* **Original Query:** Force Tracking Control Method for Robotic Ultrasound Scanning System under Soft Uncertain Environment

## Abstract
Robotic ultrasound scanning has excellent potential to reduce physician workload, obtain higher-quality images, and standardize scanning. However, maintaining stable contact force is challenging in soft, uncertain environments due to tissue deformation and patient breathing. This paper proposes an integral adaptive admittance control framework. The virtual model parameter are dynamically updated based on environment stiffness estimation. Experimental results on phantoms show that the proposed controller maintains a constant contact force with significantly reduced errors compared to standard admittance control.

## Detailed Methodology and Results
This study introduces an integral adaptive admittance control method to maintain stable contact force during automated ultrasound scanning. In soft environments with uncertain properties, tissue deformation makes tracking difficult. The proposed controller estimates the environment stiffness online and dynamically adapts the admittance parameters. A second-order virtual mass-spring-damper model is used. Experimental verification on soft phantoms demonstrates that the force tracking error (RMSE) is reduced below 0.3 N, showing significant improvement over standard non-adaptive admittance control.
