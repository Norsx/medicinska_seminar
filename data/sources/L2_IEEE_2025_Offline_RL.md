# Offline Reinforcement Learning-Based Adaptive Impedance Control for Variable Force Tracking in Robotic Ultrasound Scanning

* **ID:** L2
* **Authors:** Liang Yao, Yun Zhang, Ziqi Zhou, Yizhe Sun, Baoliang Zhao, Ziwen Wang, Peng Zhang, Faqin Lv, Pak Kin Wong, Ying Hu
* **Year:** 2025
* **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1109/robio66223.2025.11377341
* **Original Query:** Offline Reinforcement Learning-Based Adaptive Impedance Control for Variable Force Tracking in Robotic Ultrasound Scanning

## Abstract
Robotic ultrasound scanning (RUSS) has become a promising solution to standardize ultrasound (US) examinations and relieve the workload of sonographers. In many practical RUSS applications, the probe must track a time-varying force profile, especially when scanning patients with musculoskeletal pain, tumors, or other localized lesions. In such cases, conventional adaptive impedance control (AIC) is difficult to tune and often fails to generalize across different patients and anatomical regions. Moreover, directly learning a force control policy online by trial-and-error on patients is clinically unacceptable due to safety concerns. To address these challenges, we introduce an offline reinforcement learning based adaptive impedance control scheme (ORL-AIC) for variable force tracking in RUSS. The proposed controller learns a near-optimal policy from a limited set of pre-collected interaction samples, without requiring additional online exploration on patients. Phantom and in vivo experiments on a real RUSS platform demonstrate that ORLAIC achieves reduced overshoot and improved tracking accuracy compared with conventional AIC baselines, enabling safer and more patient-specific robotic US scanning.

## Summary and Conclusions
This paper addresses the design, methodology, and implementation of robotic ultrasound control. It introduces techniques to improve force regulation, visual tracking, or safety systems. Detailed analysis demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approaches in medical robotics.
