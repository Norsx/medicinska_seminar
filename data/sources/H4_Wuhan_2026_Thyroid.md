# Robotic thyroid ultrasound scanning based on adaptive force/position hybrid control

* **ID:** H4
* **Authors:** Luoshen Li, Yao Wang
* **Year:** 2026
* **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1088/1742-6596/3240/1/012037
* **Original Query:** Robotic thyroid ultrasound scanning based on adaptive force/position hybrid control

## Abstract
Abstract To address the issues of unstable contact force and operator dependence in traditional thyroid ultrasound examinations, this paper proposes a robotic ultrasound scanning method based on adaptive force/position hybrid control. A 6-DOF robotic manipulator (xArm6) integrated with a six-dimensional force sensor is employed. Considering the anatomical structure of the thyroid, a "star-pattern" scanning trajectory is designed to ensure comprehensive coverage. An admittance control algorithm based on a second-order mass-spring-damper model is implemented to regulate the contact force, setting a constant target force of 5.0 N. Experimental results on a healthy volunteer demonstrate that the system exhibits a fast step response with a settling time of approximately 0.73 s. During continuous scanning, the contact force remains stable with a Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) of less than 0.25 N, significantly improving the stability and standardization of the examination.

## Summary and Conclusions
This paper addresses the design, methodology, and implementation of robotic ultrasound control. It introduces techniques to improve force regulation, visual tracking, or safety systems. Detailed analysis demonstrates the effectiveness of the proposed approaches in medical robotics.
